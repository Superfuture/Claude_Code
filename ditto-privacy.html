Privacy Policy

Your messages stay yours.

Effective: 2026-05-16

The short version

Ditto generates reply suggestions for messages you choose to send through the iMessage extension. The text is processed in‑memory by an AI model and immediately discarded. We never store your messages, never train on them, never sell or share them, and never link them to your identity.

Who we are

Ditto is published by Joey Primiani ("we", "us"). Contact: hello@ditto.app. This policy describes how the Ditto iOS app and its iMessage extension handle your information.

What we collect

Only what is strictly required to produce a reply suggestion:

  • Message text you submit to Ditto. When you open the Ditto iMessage extension on a message, or paste a message into Ditto, the text of that message is sent to our backend, which forwards it to a large language model (Anthropic) to generate three reply options.
  • Tone selection (Funny, Flirty, Formal, Supportive).
  • An anonymous device identifier generated on first launch, used only to enforce the daily free‑tier limit. It is not tied to your name, email, Apple ID, phone number, or any other personal identifier.

We do not collect: your name, email, Apple ID, contacts, photos, location, advertising identifiers, or analytics about your behavior inside the app.

How your data is used

  • The submitted message text is sent over HTTPS to our proxy server, which adds the AI prompt and forwards the request to Anthropic's Claude API.
  • Anthropic returns three reply suggestions; we return them to your device.
  • Neither our server nor Anthropic retains the submitted text. Our server does not log message content. Anthropic processes the request under zero‑data‑retention terms and does not use the input to train models.
  • The anonymous device identifier and a daily counter are stored in Cloudflare Workers KV for up to 36 hours, solely to enforce rate limits. They are not associated with any other identifier.

On‑device data

The app stores the following on your device only, and never transmits them:

  • Your tone preference
  • The daily usage counter
  • Your subscription status (Free or Pro)
  • Optional thumbs‑up / thumbs‑down feedback on suggestions (a boolean per suggestion ID — not the text of the suggestion or the message)

Third parties

  • Anthropic, PBC processes message text to generate suggestions. Anthropic Commercial Terms · Anthropic Privacy Policy
  • Cloudflare, Inc. hosts our proxy server. Cloudflare Privacy Policy
  • Apple, Inc. handles subscription billing through the App Store. Apple Privacy Policy

No advertising networks, analytics SDKs, or marketing trackers are integrated.

In‑App Purchases

Subscriptions ($4.99/month or $29.99/year for Ditto Pro) are processed by Apple. We receive only an anonymous receipt confirming entitlement. We do not see your payment method, name, or billing address.

Children

Ditto is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has used the app, contact us and we'll delete any anonymous identifier associated with that device on request.

Your rights

Because we do not store message content or personally identifiable information, there is nothing identifying you to delete. You can:

  • Reset your anonymous device identifier at any time by deleting and reinstalling the app.
  • Stop sending text to our server by simply not using the extension.
  • Cancel a Pro subscription at any time through Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.

Residents of the EU, UK, and California: under GDPR, UK GDPR, and CCPA respectively, we provide the same minimal‑data treatment described above to everyone. Contact us at hello@ditto.app with any data‑subject request.

Security

All network requests use HTTPS (TLS 1.3). Our backend secrets (Anthropic API key, Cloudflare credentials) are stored in encrypted environment variables and never exposed to the app binary.

Changes to this policy

If we materially change how Ditto handles data, we will update this page and the effective date above. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance.
