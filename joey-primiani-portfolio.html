About
I build things at the intersection of culture, community, and technology.

I've spent my career creating digital products that people don't just use — they identify with. From designing Lady Gaga's social network to shipping a Chrome extension that Fast Company called ingenious, I've always been drawn to the hard problem of making technology feel human.

Today I run Superfuture, a creative studio focused on experimental digital products and social experiences. I work with a small number of clients on product design, creative direction, and building 0→1 consumer products.

I'm a Forbes 30 Under 30 alumnus and have been backed by Sequoia Capital, Lady Gaga, and Eric Schmidt.
